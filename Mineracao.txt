cotton /ˈkɑːtn/: a soft, white material that grows on the seeds of a tall plant and that is used to make cloth. Originally, paper was made from cotton.
rode /rəʊd/: to sit on an animal, especially a horse, and control it as it moves (montou, cavalgou, montei). He rode and taught a great number of horses. 
accent /ˈæksent/: a way of pronouncing words that occurs among the people in a particular region or country. He also speaks with a strong Australian accent.
gum /ɡʌm/: either of the areas of flesh in the mouth to which the teeth are attached (gengiva, chiclete, goma). Right. I'll chew some gum after. 
foreclosure /fɔːrˈkləʊʒər/: the act of taking control of somebody's property because they have not paid back money that they borrowed to buy it; an example of this (execução hipotecária, encerramento, exclusão, leilão, penhora, despejo). He had lost his home to foreclosure last year.
holler /ˈhɑːlər/: to shout loudly. Don't holler at me!
charity /ˈtʃærəti/: an organization for helping people in need (caridade, instituição de caridade). As always, all our celebrities play for charity. 
vouch /vaʊtʃ/: to say that (someone or something) is honest, true, or good (garantir, atestar, confirmar, testemunhar). As a victim, I can vouch it works. 
mortgage /ˈmɔːrɡɪdʒ/: a legal agreement in which a person borrows money to buy property (such as a house) and pays back the money over a period of years (hipoteca, financiamento, empréstimo). If we didn't share a mortgage, I would leave you.
