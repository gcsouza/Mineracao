wrap /ræp/: to cover something completely in paper or other material, for example when you are giving it as a present (envolver, embrulhar, enrolar, embalar). I can't really wrap my brain around any of it.
lowest /ləʊ/: mais baixo, menor, último, inferior. They began their rebuild in the lowest class.
tapestry /ˈtæpəstri/: a heavy cloth that has designs or pictures woven into it and that is used for wall hangings, curtains, etc (tapete de parede). And we were put in this tapestry room.
nursery /ˈnɜːrsəri/: a place where young children are cared for while their parents are at work. The children are at nursery three days a week.
nanny /ˈnæni/: a person whose job is to take care of young children in the children’s own home. When I was growing up, I had a nanny.
conked out /ˈkɑːŋk/: to fall asleep (adormecer, adormeci, desmaiei). I conked out early last night.
loneliest: unhappy because you have no friends or people to talk to (mais solitário, mais só, mais sozinho). It's the loneliest job in the world.
commitment /kəˈmɪtmənt/: a promise to do something or to behave in a particular way; a promise to support somebody/something; the fact of committing yourself. Learning to play the violin requires strong commitment.
creepiest /ˈkriːpi/: causing an unpleasant feeling of fear or slight horror (mais assustador, mais sinistro, mais apavorante). Of course, you would find the creepiest place in this house.
runny /ˈrʌni/: (of your nose or eyes) producing a lot of liquid, for example when you have a cold (mole, líquido, ralo, ranhoso, escorrendo). No, that's runny cheese.
