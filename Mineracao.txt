verge: a piece of grass at the edge of a path, road, etc. (beira, limite, margem, acostamento). I got a feeling that man's on the verge.
intervene: to become involved in a situation in order to improve or help it. Eventually, the army was forced to intervene.
slip away: to stop existing; to disappear or die. Their support gradually slipped away.
domineering: trying to control other people without considering their opinions or feelings. Probably you had a very domineering mother.
aspirational: wanting very much to achieve success in your career or to improve your social status and standard of living. I didn't realize you had political aspirations.
half-baked: not well planned or considered (incompleta,meia boca, imaturo, inexperiente, feito pela metade). Our half-baked ideas are all we've got. 
buzzword: a word or phrase, especially one connected with a particular subject, that has become fashionable and popular and is used a lot in newspapers, etc. (palavra de ordem). Globalization is an international buzzword.
kismet: the idea that everything that happens to you in your life is already decided and that you cannot do anything to change or control it. Come on, think of it as kismet.
