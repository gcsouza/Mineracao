endangerment /ɪnˈdeɪndʒərmənt/: the act of putting somebody/something in a situation in which they could be harmed or damaged. I want this man arrested for public endangerment.
groin /ɡrɔɪn/: the part of the body where the legs join the main part of the body, including the area around the genitals (virilha, partes baixas, zona genital, saco). The patient was punched in the groin. 
flayed /fleɪ/: to beat or whip (someone or something) in a very violent and severe way — usually used figuratively (esfolado, açoitado). You, who had been flayed alive.
acquaintance /əˈkweɪntəns/: a person that you know but who is not a close friend. And an acquaintance can become a life partner.
bounty /ˈbaʊnti/: generous actions; something provided in large quantities (generosidade, prêmio, graça)/money given as a reward (recompensa). The Romans' loss again becomes our bounty.
lend /lend/: to give something to somebody or allow them to use something that belongs to you, which they have to return to you later. Maybe Jacqueline can lend you money.
lump /lʌmp/: a piece of something hard or solid, usually without a particular shape (carolo, nó, torrão, cubo de açúcar). They found a lump under my arm. 
tread /tred/: to walk (andar, caminhar, piso, passo, pisada). But alone l cannot tread upon this path.
guzzling /ˈɡʌzl/: to drink something quickly and in large amounts. In British English it also means to eat food quickly and in large amounts. I don't want your sons guzzling my vintage wines.
bandage /ˈbændɪdʒ/: a long narrow piece of cloth used for tying around a part of the body that has been hurt in order to protect or support it. It's time to change your bandage.
