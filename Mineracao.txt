mourned: to feel and show that you are sad because somebody has died; to feel sad because something no longer exists or is no longer the same. She mourned for her lost childhood. 
syrup: a sweet liquid made from sugar and water, often used in cans of fruit. Ice cream and chocolate syrup. 
eulogy: a speech or piece of writing praising somebody/something very much. I still have to give this eulogy.
lurking: to wait somewhere secretly, especially because you are going to do something bad or illegal. I saw a man lurking in the shadows.
former: used to say what someone or something was in the past/existing in the past. This beautiful old building has been restored to its former glory.
wrestle: to fight somebody by holding them and trying to throw or force them to the ground, sometimes as a sport. She wrestled with her attacker.
shady: giving or providing shade. We went to find somewhere cool and shady to have a drink.
dewdrops: a small drop of dew or other liquid (gota de orvalho). Fresh as a dewdrop in the morning.
forbids: to order somebody not to do something; to order that something must not be done. You cannot do that. I absolutely forbid it.
defy: to refuse to obey or show respect for somebody in authority, a law, a rule, etc. I defy you to prove that I lied.
