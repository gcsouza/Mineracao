chopped /tʃɑːp/: to cut something into pieces with a sharp tool such as a knife. The furniture had been chopped to pieces.
bear /ber/: to accept or endure (something) (suportar, dar, carregar, aguentar). Almost more than a woman can bear. 
breakdown /ˈbreɪkdaʊn/: colapso, quebra, repartição, esgotamento, crise, fracasso, esgotamento nervoso. I did have a mental breakdown.
healthier /ˈhelθi/: having good health and not likely to become ill (mais saudável). People are healthier and drinking less. 
fence /fens/: a structure like a wall built outdoors usually of wood or metal that separates two areas or prevents people or animals from entering or leaving (cerca, barreira, grade, cercado). We put up a fence around our yard.
sourest /ˈsaʊər/: having a taste like that of a lemon or of fruit that is not ready to eat. You took the sourest lemon that life has to offer. and turned it into something resembling lemonade.
spurts /spɜːrt/:   an amount of liquid, flame, etc., that comes out of something suddenly (surtos, jorra, esguicha). Adults don't have growth spurts.
famine /ˈfæmɪn/: a lack of food during a long period of time in a region. Four million people are now facing famine.
pestilence /ˈpestɪləns/: ​any disease that spreads quickly and kills a lot of people (peste, praga, epidemia). After years of war and pestilence, few people remained in the city.
