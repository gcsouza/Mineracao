canopy /ˈkænəpi/: a cover that is fixed or hangs above a bed, seat, etc. as a shelter or decoration (marquise, tenda, teto, cobertura, toldo). Apparently everything moves below the great canopy.
rod /rɑːd/: a long straight piece of wood, metal or glass (barra, vara, bastão, cajado). You check people with this iron rod.
dingy /ˈdɪndʒi/: dark and dirty. The kitchen was rather dark and dingy.
fellowship /ˈfeləʊʃɪp/: a feeling of friendship between people who do things together or share an interest. Think about the church fellowship you attend.
fee /fiː/: an amount that is paid for work done by a doctor, lawyer, etc. Most family doctors receive a fee per visit.
thereof /ˌðerˈʌv/: of the thing that has been mentioned (disso, desse fato, deste, derivados, daquilo, dos mesmos). We also need to know about his family life or lack thereof.
soul /səʊl/: the spiritual part of a person, believed to exist after death. Your soul needs spiritual food - every day.
rapture /ˈræptʃər/: a feeling of extreme pleasure and happiness (êxtase, arrebatamento, entusiasmo/arrebatar). The next 100 years of relief and rapture. 
covenant: a promise to somebody, or a legal agreement, especially one to pay a regular amount of money to somebody/something (pacto, aliança, convênio, acordo). Only then will the covenant be restored. 
gigs /ɡɪɡ/: a job for a musician, an actor, etc. (shows, concertos). Maybe you should come to one of my real gigs.
