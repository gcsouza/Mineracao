rehearsals /rɪˈhɜːrsl/: time that is spent practising a play or piece of music in preparation for a public performance. I have instructions not to interrupt rehearsals.
postponed /pəʊˈspəʊn/: to decide that something which had been planned for a particular time will be done at a later time instead. The baseball game was postponed until/to tomorrow because of rain.
clashes /klæʃ/: to be in a situation in which you are fighting or disagreeing : to come into conflict with someone (confrontos, conflitos). Several were killed or wounded in the clashes. 
dentures /ˈdentʃərz/: a set of artificial teeth — usually plural. She takes out her dentures before going to bed.
onesie /ˈwʌnzi/: a piece of clothing that covers the top half of the body and the legs (macação, pijama). But this onesie was a love at first site. 
beard /bɪrd/: hair that grows on the chin and sides of somebody's face; similar hair that grows on some animals. Do you like men with beards?
goatee /ɡəʊˈtiː/: a small pointed beard (= hair growing on a man’s face) that is grown only on the chin. No one's even commented that I shaved my goatee.
chin /tʃɪn/: the part of the face below the mouth and above the neck. He had bits of food on his chin.
sapling /ˈsæplɪŋ/: a young tree (muda, rebento, broto, árvore nova). That sapling is made of pure love. 
drips /drɪp/: (of liquid) to fall in small drops. You need to stay close and watch for drips.
