merrier: very happy and cheerful : feeling or showing joy and happiness. I am merrier to die than you are to live.
withdraw: to take money out of a bank account (retirar, sacar, retirar-se). You can also withdraw invitations before travelers accept them.
mend: to repair something that has been damaged or broken so that it can be used again. Could you mend my bike for me?
purchases: to buy something. You can purchase one or many reports.
harassment: the act of annoying or worrying somebody by putting pressure on them or saying or doing unpleasant things to them. No excuse for that kind of harassment.
fallout: dangerous radioactive dust that is in the air after a nuclear explosion (consequência, precipitação, chuva radioativa). It's a real fallout of your actions. 
patsy: a weak person who is easily cheated or tricked, or who is forced to take the blame for something that somebody else has done wrong (bode expiatório). I told you before I was a patsy.
rug: a piece of thick material like a small carpet that is used for covering or decorating part of a floor. An oriental rug.
newlyweds: a person who has recently got married. But the marriage certificate that the newlyweds receive is real.
hustle: to make somebody move quickly by pushing them in a rough aggressive way (agitação, alvoroço, empurrar, apressar, tumulto). But I know a hustle when I see one.
